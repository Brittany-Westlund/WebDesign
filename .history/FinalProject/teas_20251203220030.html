Tea Recipe

Marigold

The Flower of Golden Warmth

Critters who love it:

  • Deer
  • Butterfly
  • Bird

Toxic to:

  • Snail
  • Squirrel
  • Monkey

Marigold blooms carry a gentle, sun-warm glow even under moonlight. Their petals brew into a bright, comforting tea that lifts tired thoughts and warms the spirit. Deer and butterflies are especially fond of marigolds, often resting near their soft golden light.

Goldenrod

The Flower of Steady Courage

Critters who love it:

  • Deer
  • Squirrel
  • Bird

Toxic to:

  • Snail
  • Beetle
  • Monkey

Goldenrod blooms shimmer with a bold, amber glow that never wavers, even in deep forest shade. Their tea has a grounding, steadying warmth that clears the mind and strengthens resolve. Deer and birds favor goldenrod for its brave, bright energy, while squirrels enjoy the extra pep it gives them.

Strawberry

The Flower of Sweet Cheer

Critters who love it:

  • Monkey
  • Squirrel
  • Bird

Toxic to:

  • Snail
  • Beetle

Strawberry blossoms carry a bright, playful sweetness that lingers in the air like a soft laugh. Their tea is lively and uplifting, bringing a spark of joy to even the quietest morning. Squirrels adore its sweetness, birds love the fresh fruit scent, and monkey-folk are always drawn to its spirited, cheerful warmth.

Tea Rose

The Flower of Quiet Heart

Critters who love it:

  • Snail
  • Butterfly
  • Deer

Toxic to:

  • Monkey
  • Squirrel
  • Beetle

Tea roses hold a gentle, fragrant calm, their petals collecting dew like tiny pearls. Brewed, they create a soft, soothing tea that eases tight thoughts and brings a quiet warmth to the heart. Snails adore their cool, tender petals, and butterflies drift toward their sweet, peaceful scent.
